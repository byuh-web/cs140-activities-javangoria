Experience Shibuya

Artistic shots of Shibuya's iconic crossing continue to feature on the pages of global fashion and travel magazines, and for good reason. The chaotic, yet miraculously organized crossing has become a symbol of Tokyo's dynamism, and represents Shibuya—Japan's trend-setting powerhouse dedicated to fashion and arts.

I love exploring Shibuya because it perfectly captures the energy of modern Tokyo. There's always something new to discover, from hidden art galleries in Oku-Shibuya to the latest fashion trends in Shibuya109. The contrast between the massive crowds at the scramble crossing and the quiet, hip bars in backstreets creates an unforgettable urban experience.

Essential Tips for Shibuya

  • Jostle in the crossing—Shibuya's iconic intersection
  • Cutting-edge fashion and modern lifestyle shopping
  • Movies, music, books - immerse yourself in Shibuya's art scene

How to Get There

In addition to JR Lines, Shibuya Station is also on the Keio Inokashira Line, the Tokyu Toyoko Line, Tokyu Den-en-toshi Line, and Tokyo Metro's Ginza, Hanzomon and Fukutoshin Lines.

Travel Times to Shibuya Station:

  1. From Haneda Airport: 55 minutes by Limousine Bus, or 45 minutes by train.
  2. From Narita Airport: one hour 55 minutes by Limousine Bus, or one hour 40 minutes by train.
  3. From Shinjuku Station: 7 minutes on the JR Yamanote Line.
  4. From Tokyo Station: 23 minutes on the JR Yamanote Line.

Shopping Hub for Fashionistas

The streets of Shibuya continue to be responsible for global fashion trend-setting.

  • Shibuya109: Mecca for fashion-conscious teenagers
  • Shibuya Hikarie & Seibu: Sophisticated fashion and dining complexes
  • Jinnan area: Distinctive select shops
  • Hands or Loft: Modern lifestyle megastores for creators
  • Don Quijote: Quirky souvenirs and discount goods

Shibuya Sky & Entertainment City

Shibuya's dynamic and ever-changing nature brings new commercial facilities.

  • Shibuya Sky: 360° panoramic view from 230m height
  • Shibuya Parco: Center for art, culture, food, and manga
  • MIYASHITA PARK: Local food from all over Japan
  • Shibuya Yokocho: Gourmet specialties and karaoke bars

A New Center for Anime

Based on Shibuya's reputation as a popular area for young people and aim to become more of an international city, the Tokyo Anime Center in DNP PLAZA SHIBUYA opened in April 2021, as a new center for anime culture.

Anime & Manga with Shibuya Settings:

  • "Idolmaster Cinderella Girls"
  • "Jujutsu Kaisen" (Shibuya Incident arc)
  • Anime and game floors on the 6th floor of Parco
