Natural Products and Handicrafts

Vigan Empanada

Crispy rice flour shell filled with longganisa, green papaya, munggo, and egg. A beloved Ilocano merienda, best with spicy sukang iloko.

Pili Nuts (Que Rica)

Hand-harvested in Bicol, these premium nuts are crafted in small batches roasted, caramelized, or coated with honey. A decadent export-quality treat.

Turon (Banana Lumpia)

Saba bananas and jackfruit strips wrapped in spring roll pastry, caramelized with brown sugar. Classic street food, crispy and sweet.

Sapin-Sapin

Layered glutinous rice cake with coconut milk, colored pink, white, and yellow. Topped with latik,a staple at any Filipino celebration.

Bukayo

Shredded young coconut slow-cooked in brown sugar until chewy and caramelized. A traditional dessert often sold in markets wrapped in coconut leaves.

T'nalak Handicraft

Handwoven by T'boli women in Lake Sebu. Made from abaca fibers dyed with natural hues, each pattern tells a dream, no two pieces are alike.
